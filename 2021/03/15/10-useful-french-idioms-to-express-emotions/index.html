10 useful French idioms to express emotions

Hello everyone, emotions and feelings are part of our communication. In today’s episode you’ll learn how to translate your emotions into expressions in French.

Below are 10 common French idioms with explanations and examples that you can use to sound more French and impress native speakers.

#1 – Être dans ses petits souliers

Meaning: to feel uncomfortable

Example: Il est vraiment dans ses petits souliers ce soir. 

(He’s really uncomfortable tonight.)

#2 – Prendre ombrage

Meaning: to feel worry

Example: Je suis parti content, sans en prendre ombrage.

(I left happy, without taking offense.)

#3 – Battre la chamade

Meaning: to be in the shock of a strong emotion, to beat widely (heart)

Example: Quand tu m’appelles au téléphone, mon cœur bat la chamade.

(When you call me on the telephone, my heart races.)

#4 – Avoir le béguin pour quelqu’un

Meaning: to be in love with someone

Example: J’ai un sacré béguin pour vous. 

(I have a heck of a crush on you.)

#5 – Être le canard boiteux

Meaning: to be a black sheep, to be a person in charge of a function but not able to take care of it properly. 

Example: Je veux pas de canard boiteux dans l’équipe. 

(I don’t want a black sheep on the team.)

#6 – Avoir le cafard

Meaning: to be depressed

Example: Quand tu as le cafard, je t’emmène voir un film, comme aujourd’hui. 

(When you have the blues, I take you to see a movie, like today.)

#7 – Être tout feu tout flamme

Meaning: to be aflame with passion, to be fired with enthusiasm

Example: Il était tout feu tout flamme à l’idée de participer aux prochaines élections.

(He was aflame with passion because he wanted to get involved in the election.)

#8 – Prendre la mouche

Meaning: to get upset, to take offense

Example: J’essaie de ne pas prendre la mouche.

(I’m trying not to get upset.)

#9 – Être médusé

Meaning: to be amazed

Example: Je suis vraiment médusé  par son approche.

(I am really amazed by his approach.)

#10 – Les bras m’en tombent

Meaning: I’m very surprised, I don’t know what to say.

Example: À cette nouvelle, les bras m’en tombent. 

(With this news, I’m very surprised.)
