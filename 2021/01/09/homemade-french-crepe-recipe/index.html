Homemade French Crepe Recipe

In France, despite the fact that crepes are eaten all year round, there is a feast of crepes which takes place on the 2nd February every year. It is also known as La Chandeleur (Candlemas) – the occasion that families and friends make crepes and enjoy them together. 

It is believed that while holding a coin in your left hand, you flip the crepe in the air with your right hand, if you successfully make the crepe land properly back in the pan, it brings you luck, richness and prosperity all year long (“bonne chance” in French). If the crepe falls, it brings bad luck (“malchance” in French). 

In this article, I’m going to share a simple recipe and some tips for making extremely delicious French crepes (homemade crepes). Basically, crepes are good ideas for all family members’ breakfast, brunch, lunch, dinner, or dessert. 

Crepes and pancakes taste quite much similar, there is a saying that “Crepes are the European cousin of American pancakes.” However, crepes are larger, thinner than pancakes, because the batter of making crepes is also thinner without any leavening agents.

There are two types of crepes: sweet crepes and savory crepes. The sweet crepes are usually served with sweet fillings such as: fresh fruits, fruit jams, chocolate (or Nutella), whipped cream, yogurt, syrup, honey… The savory crepes are served with savory fillings like ham, cheese, bacon, fried eggs, salads, mushrooms, herbs…

Let’s discover how to make homemade French crepes right away!

🌸Equipment: 1 large mixing bowl, 1 whisk, 1 frying pan (a non-stick pan), 1 ladle

🌸 Ingredients: 

1 cup (125g) all-purpose flour or 1 cup (120g) whole wheat flour 

2 eggs

1 cup (250 ml) milk and 1 tablespoon (15g) sugar 

1/4 cup (50g) butter

25ml coconut cream or 1 tablespoon (15g) coconut powder 

3 tablespoon (45g) Nestle vanilla cereal powder 

3 tablespoons (45g) unsweetened cocoa powder (if you want to make chocolate crepes)

1 tablespoon (15g) Chia seeds

Fillings: fresh fruit, fruit jams, chocolate, ham, cheese, bacon, fried eggs, salads, mushrooms…

🌸Here are some useful tips for perfect homemade crepes: 

+/ Using butter is better than using cooking oil when making crepes. Clearly, butter is an indispensable ingredient to create an unique flavor for crepes. I often use the butter Fruit d’Or to create the aroma for the crepes as well as add Omega 3 in the diet of family members.

+/ It is not necessary to have Chia seeds in the crepes. Since Chia seeds are quite nutritious, I often put them in the batter.

+/ Nestle cereal powder is also an optional ingredient. Nestle cereal powder consists of 8 cereals (wheat, triticale, barley, rye, rice, corn, spelt, whole oat) with a lot of nutrients and fiber like Chia seeds. In addition, I find that when there is Nestle cereal powder, the crepes’ flavor is quite similar to the crepes’ flavor that I had in a restaurant in Paris once, so I usually add this powder when making crepes at home.

🌸 Instructions: 

Step 1

Whisk the dry ingredients: flour, Nestle cereal powder, (cocoa powder), coconut powder, Chia seeds, sugar in a big mixing bowl together for 30 seconds. If you want to make savory crepes, don’t add sugar to the mixture.

Step 2 

Add fresh milk to the dry mixture above, use a whisk to mix well for a minute.

Step 3

Beat 2 eggs into the mixture, use a whisk to mix until completely smooth (about 2-3 minutes). If you find your mixture is too thick, you can add a little bit of milk.

Step 4

Cover the bowl with food wrap, let the crepe batter rest in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes to allow the liquids to saturate the starches (in a rush 30 minutes will probably do).

Step 5

While waiting for the dough to rest, we make fillings to serve with crepe.

You can choose any fruits, jams or any savory fillings to enjoy with your crepes. For example: 

~ With grapes (preferably seedless grapes), raw vegetables, herbs we should wash and soak in salt water for 15 minutes, then drain them.

~ With other fruits, we wash, peel and slice (with banana, persimmon…), dice (with kiwi, strawberry…)

~ With ham, bacon, we can put whole round pieces on the crepes without chopping.

~ With shrimps, we peel and devein them and then steam them. 

… 

Step 6 – Make crepes 

+/ Heat the pan to medium heat. When the pan is hot, brush the surface with butter.

+/ Give the batter a quick whisk again before using.

+/ Use a ladle to pour/ scoop the batter into the pan, using approximately 2 tablespoons for each crepe.

+/ Tilt the pan with a circular motion so that the batter coats the surface evenly and thinly as possible.

+/ Cook the first side for about 1 minute or until the edges of the crepe start to get golden brown, use a flat spatula to flip over the crepe. And cook it on the other side for about 30-45 seconds, then transfer it to a plate. 

+/ Continue to do the same with the rest of the batter, stack the crepes onto the plate.

Step 7 – Serve the crepes with your favorite fillings

Here are some crepe filling ideas you can try:

#1: Fresh fruit crepe with kiwi and seedless grapes, you can add a little fruit jam to decorate it like a Christmas tree

#2: Fresh fruit crepe with kiwi and banana

#3: Fresh fruit crepe with persimmon, black grape and kiwi

#4: Crepe rolled with jam from cherry, strawberry, raspberry, redcurrant

#5: Chocolate-fruit crepe with apple, kiwi, pomegranate (you can dust with coconut powder on top)

#6: Bow-shaped chocolate crepe with pear, apple, kiwi, pomegranate, seedless black currant (you can dust with coconut powder on top)

#7: Chocolate crepe with emmental cheese (you can add a few peanuts, and some fruits)

#8: Chocolate crepe with the laughing cow cheese (you can add your favorite fruits: persimmon, apple, pear …)

#9: Chocolate crepe rolled with fresh cheese

#10: Savory crepe filled with steamed shrimp, salad, tomato, emmental cheese

#11: Savory crepe filled with sausage, salad, tomato, emmental cheese

I hope that you find this homemade French crepe recipe useful. Well, enjoy your crepes!💝
