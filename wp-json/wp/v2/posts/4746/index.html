The horse (\u201cle cheval\u201d in French) is a one-toed hoofed herbivorous mammal. Not only the animal is domesticated by humans, but also its name is used in many common expressions in French language. Today we are going to discover French expressions with the word \u201ccheval\u201d.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#1 – Un rem\u00e8de de cheval<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fDefinition : Strong medicine (a particularly strong and violent remedy, but which is intended to be effective)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fSynonym : Une m\u00e9decine de cheval<\/em><\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fExample : \u00c9coutez, donnez-moi un rem\u00e8de de cheval contre la douleur.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

(Listen, give me strong medicine against my pain.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#2 – Une sant\u00e9 de cheval<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fDefinition : Excellent health<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fExample : Il faut avoir une sant\u00e9 de cheval pour \u00e9tudier et travailler efficacement.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

(It is necessary to have excellent health to study and to work effectively.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#3 – Un travail de cheval<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fDefinition : A very painful task<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fExample : C\u2019est un travail de cheval.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

(It\u2019s a very painful task.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#4 – Un grand cheval<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fDefinition : A tall, masculine-looking woman<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fExample : Ils pensent qu\u2019elle est un grand cheval.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

(They think that she is a tall, masculine-looking woman.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#5 – Un cheval de troie <\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fDefinition : An evil gift<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fExample : Elle m\u2019a donn\u00e9 un cadeau mal\u00e9fique.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

(She gave me an evil gift.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#6 – Un cheval de retour <\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fDefinition : A repeat offender<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fExample : Il est vraiment un cheval de retour.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

(He is really a repeat offender.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#7 – \u00catre \u00e0 cheval sur <\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fDefinition : To be very demanding, to be very strict on something, to attach great importance on something <\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fExample : Elle est \u00e0 cheval sur la  politesse. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

(For her, politeness is very important.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#8 – Ne pas \u00eatre un mauvais cheval<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fDefinition : to be kind, to be gentle, not to be such a bad sort<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fExample : Il est vrai qu\u2019elle n\u2019est pas un mauvais cheval.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

(It\u2019s true that she\u2019s kind.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#9 – Avoir mang\u00e9 du cheval<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fDefinition : To have unusual energy, to show unusual energy<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fExample : Pourquoi t\u2019as mang\u00e9 du cheval ?<\/p>\n\n\n\n

(Why did you have unusual energy?)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#10 – Avoir une fi\u00e8vre de cheval<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fDefinition : To have a very high fever, to have a carabine fever<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\ud83d\udc49This expression indicates that a person has a very high temperature, often around 40\u00b0.<\/em><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fExample :  Je suis arriv\u00e9 l\u00e0-bas avec une fi\u00e8vre de cheval.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

(I arrived there with a very high fever.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#11 – Avoir un cheval de bataille<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fDefinition : To have a hobby-horse, to frequently talk or complain about a subject, topic, or issue in which one is excessively interested<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fExample : Ce fut le cheval de bataille de la commission des budgets.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

(This was the hobby-horse of the budget Committee.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#12 –  Manger avec les chevaux de bois <\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fDefinition : To have nothing to eat, to fast<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fExample : Le frigo est vide, je dois manger avec les chevaux de bois. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

(The fridge is empty, I have nothing to eat.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#13 – L\u00e2cher les chevaux<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fDefinition : Give it all, go all the way<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fExample : Il faut l\u00e2cher les chevaux pour atteindre nos objectifs plus \u00e9lev\u00e9s. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

(We must go all the way to achieve our higher goals.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#14 – Changer un cheval borgne pour un aveugle<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fDefinition : To make a fool’s bargain, to set aside his own solution, albeit imperfect, for another solution that is certainly more catastrophic<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fSynonym : Troquer son cheval borgne contre un aveugle<\/em><\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fExample : De toute fa\u00e7on, pour elle, c’\u00e9tait changer son cheval borgne pour un aveugle.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

(Anyway, for her, it was a fool’s bargain.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#15 – Miser sur le bon cheval<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fDefinition : To make the right choice<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fAntonym : Miser sur le mauvais cheval <\/em><\/strong>(Don’t make the right choice, bet on someone who leads you to failure)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fExample : Mon petit, tu as mis\u00e9 sur le bon cheval.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

(My dear, you make the right choice.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#16 – Monter sur ses grands chevaux<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fDefinition : To get angry very quickly, to get angry in a stormy way<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fExample : Mon cousin monte sur vos grands chevaux pour de petites choses.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

(My cousin gets angry very quickly for small things.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#17 – Fermer l’\u00e9curie une fois les chevaux enfuis <\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fDefinition : To react too late, to wait for something to happen to protect oneself from it<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fExample : Il ne faut pas fermer l’\u00e9curie une fois les chevaux enfuis.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

(It should not react too late.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#18 – C’est la mort du petit cheval.<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fDefinition : This is the end. It is very serious.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fExample : Sinon, le reste de l’ann\u00e9e, c’est la mort du petit cheval pour tout le monde.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

(Otherwise, the rest of the year is the end for everyone.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#19 – Cela ne se trouve pas sous le pas d’un cheval.<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fDefinition : It is difficult to obtain something. It is rare, very difficult to find something.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fSynonym : Cela ne se trouve pas sous les sabots d’un cheval. \u00c7a ne se trouve pas sous le pied d’un cheval.<\/em><\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fExample : Selon la directrice, \u00e7a ne se trouve pas sous le pas d’un cheval.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

(According to the director, it is very difficult.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#20 – \u00c0 cheval donn\u00e9, on ne regarde pas les dents.<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fDefinition : Don’t look a gift horse in the mouth. (You must always be happy with a gift received, you should not criticize a gift even if it has a defect.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fSynonym : \u00c0 cheval donn\u00e9, on ne regarde pas la bouche. \u00c0 cheval donn\u00e9, on ne regarde pas la bride.<\/em><\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fExample : Je me suis dit qu’il valait mieux pas trop poser de questions : ce voyage \u00e9tait un cadeau, apr\u00e8s tout, et \u00e0 cheval donn\u00e9, on ne regarde pas les dents !<\/p>\n\n\n\n

(I told myself that it was better not to ask too many questions: this trip was a gift, after all, don’t look a gift horse in the mouth!)<\/p>\n\n\n\n
