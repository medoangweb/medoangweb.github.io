In France, despite the fact that crepes are eaten all year round, there is a feast of crepes which takes place on the 2nd February every year. It is also known as La Chandeleur (Candlemas) – the occasion that families and friends make crepes and enjoy them together. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

It is believed that while holding a coin in your left hand, you flip the crepe in the air with your right hand, if you successfully make the crepe land properly back in the pan, it brings you luck, richness and prosperity all year long (\u201cbonne chance\u201d in French). If the crepe falls, it brings bad luck (\u201cmalchance\u201d in French). <\/p>\n\n\n\n

In this article, I\u2019m going to share a simple recipe and some tips for making extremely delicious French crepes (homemade crepes). Basically, crepes are good ideas for all family members\u2019 breakfast, brunch, lunch, dinner, or dessert. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

Crepes and pancakes taste quite much similar, there is a saying that \u201cCrepes are the European cousin of American pancakes.\u201d However, crepes are larger, thinner than pancakes, because the batter of making crepes is also thinner without any leavening agents.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

There are two types of crepes: sweet crepes and savory crepes. <\/strong>The sweet crepes are usually served with sweet fillings such as: fresh fruits, fruit jams, chocolate (or Nutella), whipped cream, yogurt, syrup, honey\u2026 The savory crepes are served with savory fillings like ham, cheese, bacon, fried eggs, salads, mushrooms, herbs\u2026<\/p>\n\n\n\n
