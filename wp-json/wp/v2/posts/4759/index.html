      \ud83c\udf40Next, we will see the 5 expressions related to chance or luck in French <\/p>\n\n\n\n

      #1 – Toucher du bois <\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

      \u2714Meaning: To ward off bad luck<\/p>\n\n\n\n

      #2 – Croiser les doigts<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

      \u2714Meaning: To make the most ardent wishes for the success, for being lucky <\/p>\n\n\n\n

      #3 – Merde ! <\/strong>Or: Bonne merde ! <\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

      \u2714Meaning: Good luck!<\/p>\n\n\n\n

      #4 – Jamais deux sans trois !<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

      \u2714Meaning: Something that has already happened twice can happen a third time. That is to say, the good news follows one another, or the misfortunes repeat themselves.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

      #5 – Le hasard fait bien les choses.<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

      \u2714Meaning: As luck would have it. It means that something happens randomly and has a positive effect.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
