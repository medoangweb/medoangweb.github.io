Idiomatic expressions are used everyday by French people. In this article, there are some interesting French expressions with \u201cla lune\u201d to get you talking like a native. Let\u2019s start right away!<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#1 – Lunaire <\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0f<\/strong>Signification: an adjective which evokes the moon by its desert aspect, its pale clarity, its unreal character<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714Example: Un paysage lunaire est un paysage clair et d\u00e9sertique.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

(A lunar landscape is a clear, desert one.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#2 – Lunatique <\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0f<\/strong>Signification: an adjective to talk about a person whose mood is changeable and unpredictable<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714Example: Il est plut\u00f4t lunatique. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

(He\u2019s rather temperamental.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#3 – Lune de miel <\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0f<\/strong>Signification: honeymoon – the first stages of marriage, made of happy love <\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0fSynonym: voyage de noce<\/em><\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714Example: Ils se marient et passent leur lune de miel \u00e0 Moscou.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

(They get married and spend their honeymoon in Moscow.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#4 – Lune de mer <\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0f<\/strong>Signification: a type of silvery fish whose body is shaped like a flattened disc. It lives in warm seas and can reach 2 meters in length.\ud83d\ude2e<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714Synonym: poisson-lune<\/em><\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714Example: C’est la premi\u00e8re fois que je vois cette lune de mer.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

(This is the first time that I see this silvery fish.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#5 – Vieilles lunes <\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0f<\/strong>Signification: old-fashioned things, out-of-fashion ideas<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714Example: Lorsque le sujet des jeunes arrive sur la table, leur rapport au travail est, sans doute, l\u2019une des vieilles lunes les plus agit\u00e9es.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

(When the subject of young people arrives on the table, their relationship to work is, without doubt, one of the most agitated old-fashioned things.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#6 – Une face de pleine lune <\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0f<\/strong>Signification: a round face, a chubby face<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714Example: Elle a une face de pleine lune. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

(She has a chubby face.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#7 – Con comme la lune<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0f<\/strong>Signification: a particularly stupid person.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714Synonym: Avoir un quartier de lune dans la t\u00eate<\/em><\/strong>, b\u00eate comme ses pieds<\/em><\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714Example: Moi, je le trouve con comme la lune. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

(Me, I find him particularly stupid.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#8 – En demi-lune <\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0f<\/strong>Signification: half-moon, fan-shaped, shaped like a crescent<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714Example: Dans un disque de p\u00e2te feuillet\u00e9e, d\u00e9coupez \u00e0 l’emporte pi\u00e8ce un morceau de p\u00e2te en demi-lune ou circulaire. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

(Using a pastry cutter, cut a half-moon or circular piece of dough from a puff pastry disc)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#9 – \u00catre bien lun\u00e9 <\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0f<\/strong>Signification: to be in a good mood, to be cheerful<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714Synonym: \u00catre dans une bonne lune<\/em><\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714Antonym: \u00catre mal lun\u00e9<\/em><\/strong>, \u00eatre dans une mauvaise lune<\/em><\/strong> (to be in a bad mood)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714Example: Ma petite s\u0153ur est mal lun\u00e9e aujourd\u2019hui. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

(My little sister is in a bad mood today.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#10 – \u00catre dans la lune <\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0f<\/strong>Signification: to be distracted, to be a dreamer, to be lost in thought<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714Antonym: Avoir les pieds sur terre<\/em><\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714Example: Elle est toujours dans la lune, cette fille.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

(She’s always got her head in the clouds, that girl.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#11 – Demander la lune \/ Promettre la lune <\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0f<\/strong>Signification: to ask or to promise impossible things, unachievable actions<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714Example: Nous n\u2019allons pas vous demander la lune. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

(We are not going to ask you for impossible things.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#12 – D\u00e9crocher la lune <\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0f<\/strong>Signification: to obtain something that seemed impossible, unachievable<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714Synonym: Prendre la lune avec ses dents<\/em><\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714Example: Nos produits sont peut-\u00eatre pos\u00e9s au sol, mais notre ambition, c’est de d\u00e9crocher la lune ! <\/p>\n\n\n\n

(Our products may be floor-based, but our ambitions are sky-high!)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#13 – Tomber dans la lune <\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0f<\/strong>Signification: to be surprised by an unforeseen event<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714Example: Vous avez l’air de tomber de la lune.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

(You seemed surprised.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#14 – Aboyer \u00e0 la lune <\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0f<\/strong>Signification: to shout in vain against someone<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714Synonyme: Hurler \u00e0 la lune<\/em><\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714Example: Je n\u2019ai jamais vu \u00e7a, j\u2019ai l\u2019impression d\u2019aboyer \u00e0 la lune !<\/p>\n\n\n\n

(I’ve never seen that, I feel like shouting in vain!)<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#15 – Faire voir la lune en plein midi <\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0f<\/strong>Signification: to make someone believe in implausible things, to lie, to abuse someone’s gullibility<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714Example: Il ne faut pas faire voir la lune en plein midi. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

(You should not lie).<\/p>\n\n\n\n

#16 – Voir la lune en plein jour <\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714\ufe0f<\/strong>Signification: to believe implausible things<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u2714Example: Malheureusement, elle croit tout ce qu\u2019il lui raconte, elle voit la lune en plein jour. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

(Unfortunately, she believes everything he tells her, she believes all implausible things.)<\/p>\n\n\n\n
