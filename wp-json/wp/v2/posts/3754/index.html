BOB RETURNS HOME WITH BAD NEWS<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Bob tells his wife Susan that he lost his job. Susan suggests that he start his own business. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

Susan: What’s the matter,dear? <\/p>\n\n\n\n

Bob: Susan, I got canned today at work. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

Susan: But Bob, you were Peter’s right-hand man! <\/p>\n\n\n\n

Bob: Yes, and he stabbed me in the back. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

 Susan: Keep your chin up. Maybe he’ll change his mind and take you back. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

Bob: When pigs fly! Once he makes up his mind, he never changes it. Besides, I told him off. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

Susan: Look on the bright side: you won’t have to set eyes on Peter ever again. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

Bob: Thank goodness for that! <\/p>\n\n\n\n

Susan: Hang in there. I’m sure you won’t be out of work for long. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

Bob: In the mean time, we’ll have to live from hand to mouth. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

Susan: Don’t get too stressed out,Bob.  We’ll make ends meet. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

Bob: I can always get a job at McDonald’s as a last resort. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

Susan: I don’t think they’re hiring right now. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

Bob: If worse comes to worst, we can sell our home and move into a tent. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

Susan:Let’s think big! Maybe you can start your own business. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

Bob: Easier said than done! <\/p>\n\n\n\n

    \ud83c\udf40Moving into a new home is easier said than done. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

    => easier said than done: n\u00f3i d\u1ec5 h\u01a1n l\u00e0m<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    \ud83c\udf40Lisa is a lousy secretary. She deserves to get canned! <\/p>\n\n\n\n

    4. (to) hang in there: to persevere; to not give up <\/p>\n\n\n\n

    => (to) hang in there: h\u00e3y ki\u00ean tr\u00ec, \u0111\u1eebng b\u1ecf cu\u1ed9c<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    8. (to) live from hand to mouth: to barely have enough money tosurvive <\/p>\n\n\n\n

    \ud83c\udf40George is really poor. He lives from hand to mouth. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

    => (to) live from hand to mouth: ngh\u00e8o<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    9. (to) look on the bright side: to be optimistic; to think about the positive part or aspect of a situation <\/p>\n\n\n\n

    \ud83c\udf40You lost your job? Look on the bright side, now you’ll have more free time! <\/p>\n\n\n\n

    => (to) look on the bright side: H\u00e3y l\u1ea1c quan l\u00ean.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    \ud83c\udf40If you can’t make ends meet, you’ll need to start spending less. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

    => (to) make ends meet: trang tr\u1ea3i cu\u1ed9c s\u1ed1ng<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    11. out of work: unemployed; not working <\/p>\n\n\n\n

    \ud83c\udf40Bob is out of work.Do you know any body who might want to hire him? <\/p>\n\n\n\n

    => out of work: th\u1ea5t nghi\u1ec7p<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    12. right-hand man: the most helpful assistant or employee <\/p>\n\n\n\n

    13. (to) set eyes on: to look at; to see for the first time <\/p>\n\n\n\n

    => (to) set eyes on: g\u1eb7p l\u1ea7n \u0111\u1ea7u ti\u00ean<\/p>\n\n\n\n
